Who I Am

My focus is not on building models in isolation, but on designing intelligence that can operate responsibly inside complex, real-world environments.

I am drawn to problems where technology meets ambiguity — where systems must reason, adapt, and act under constraints rather than ideal conditions.

The Environments That Shaped Me

Much of my work has been shaped by large, distributed, and operationally critical systems. These are environments where:

Scale matters
Human decisions carry consequences
Automation must earn trust
Failure is rarely academic

Working closely with enterprise and public-sector contexts has taught me that most hard problems are not technical alone. They are organizational, behavioral, and systemic.

How This Shaped My Thinking

Over time, this led me to a few strong beliefs:

Autonomy without governance is fragile

Intelligence without evaluation is unreliable

Automation without human judgment is incomplete

Good AI systems are not defined by how clever they are, but by how well they fit into the realities of human organizations. Designing for accountability, observability, and decision clarity matters more than optimizing for novelty.

What I Work On

Today, I focus on designing and experimenting with:

Agentic AI systems

That operate across workflows

AI for service management and operations

Where decisions must be timely and explainable

Evaluation and guardrails

That keep AI useful under pressure

Reference architectures and demos

That translate ideas into something teams can reason about

This work often sits between strategy and execution — where ideas must survive contact with real systems, people, and constraints.

Credibility, Quietly

  • Strong preference for systems that can be understood, governed, and trusted
