In enterprise environments, intelligence without control is a liability. Autonomy without governance erodes trust. And systems that cannot explain or correct themselves eventually fail at scale.

I approach AI system design as a decision-making problem, not a modeling problem. That means asking questions like:

  • Where should autonomy begin — and where must it stop?
  • How are decisions evaluated, overridden, or reversed?
  • What happens when the system is confidently wrong?
  • Who remains accountable when humans and machines act together?

My thinking is shaped by operating in environments where:

  • Failure has real business and societal impact
  • Constraints are non-negotiable
  • Tradeoffs must be made explicit, not hidden behind abstractions
